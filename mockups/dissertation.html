Evaluation Form Information

Dear Faculty - Thank you for participating in the pilot implementation phase of Duke Trainee Tracking Tool (T3).

T3 Overview: T3 is a web-based application being developed by the Office of Biomedical Graduate Education (OBGE) that documents and monitors progress of trainees enrolled in SoM biomedical research PhD programs. T3 captures critical elements of the training experience: lab rotations, affiliation with faculty mentor, preliminary examination, annual committee meetings, and dissertation defense. T3 provides ongoing data collection and utilizes competency-based learning assessment aligned to major milestone events. The expectation is that student competencies will improve over the various stages of training. T3 will allow deficiencies or weaknesses in specific learning areas to be identified early, so trainees have opportunities to attain higher levels of mastery through the remainder of the training period. T3 offers transparency of expectations for students and faculty and provides clear, consistent, and standardized metrics for evaluating trainee development and progress toward degree.

Evaluation Form: Each member of the committee will access their individual evaluation form via common link provided. Faculty will sign into T3 using NetID and password. The evaluation form is divided into 6 sections: Abstract, Background, Methods, Results, Discussion, Oral Exam. The first 5 sections (Abstract, Background, Methods, Results, Discussion) refer to both the written and oral exam. In each of the 6 sections, student learning outcomes will be scored based on the following scale: Poor (1), Insufficient (2), Competent (3), Very Good (4), and Superlative (5), N/A (6).

The following guidelines may aid in scoring:

Poor (1) Weak performance; trainee exhibits little to no competency and requires additional foundational training
Insufficient (2) More weakness than strength; trainee exhibits inadequate competency that denotes significant room for improvement
Competent (3) More strength than weakness; trainee exhibits reasonable or adequate competency with room for improvement
Very good (4) Little weakness and significant strength; trainee exhibits a high level of competency with modest room for improvement
Superlative (5) Extremely strong; trainee exhibits the highest level of competency

Guidelines for Determining Pass/Fail ( Exam):

Pass: An average score (across all sections and student learning outcomes) of greater than or equal to 4.00 for each committee member constitutes an affirmative (Pass) vote. Passing the requires at least 4 affirmative (Pass) votes.

Fail: If the student receives fewer than 4 affirmative votes, the committee should score the exam as a Fail. A student who fails the may apply, with the unanimous consent of the examination committee and the DGS, for the privilege of a second examination to be taken between three and six months (in case of prelim) or at least six months (in case of dissertation) after the date of the first. Successful completion of the second examination requires the affirmative vote of all original committee members.
